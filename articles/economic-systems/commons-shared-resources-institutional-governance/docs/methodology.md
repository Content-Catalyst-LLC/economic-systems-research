# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models commons governance as a relationship among resource stock dynamics, extraction pressure, regeneration, monitoring capacity, rule clarity, legitimacy, access justice, enclosure risk, and institutional integrity.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized renewable resource scenarios.
2. Creates user extraction profiles with different access rights and compliance tendencies.
3. Creates governance scenarios for open access, community governance, state regulation, polycentric governance, enclosure, and capture.
4. Simulates shared resource stock trajectories under different governance conditions.
5. Calculates harvest-regeneration balances and depletion risk.
6. Estimates compliance as a function of monitoring, rule clarity, legitimacy, sanctions, and capture risk.
7. Evaluates access justice and enclosure risk.
8. Simulates institutional integrity over time.
9. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
10. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Commons are not simply unmanaged open-access resources; they are institutionally governed shared resources.
- Shared extraction can become unsustainable when aggregate harvest persistently exceeds regeneration.
- Governance depends on rules, boundaries, monitoring, sanctions, conflict resolution, local knowledge, legitimacy, and institutional upkeep.
- Enclosure can preserve a resource while undermining access justice, or it can enable private rent extraction.
- Polycentric governance can reduce single-point failure when local, regional, and national institutions are coordinated.
- Institutional failure can precede visible ecological collapse.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with resource-specific ecological data, hydrological records, fisheries stock assessments, forest inventories, remote-sensing data, local institutional records, time-use data, governance-quality indicators, and participatory monitoring.
