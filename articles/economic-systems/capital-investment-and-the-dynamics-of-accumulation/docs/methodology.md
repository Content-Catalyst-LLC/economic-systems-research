# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models capital, investment, and accumulation as problems of stock-flow dynamics, depreciation, maintenance, public and private discounting, ownership claims, financial allocation, investment direction, and sustainable-development capacity.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized capital-stock and investment scenarios.
2. Simulates capital accumulation paths under different investment and depreciation conditions.
3. Calculates capital intensity and investment rates.
4. Compares project NPV under private and public discount rates.
5. Tracks maintenance backlog under deferred upkeep.
6. Models output-claim splits among wages, profits, taxes, and rents.
7. Evaluates finance direction across productive, speculative, extractive, and resilient investment.
8. Evaluates sustainable-investment scenarios using resilience, ecological, public value, and maintenance indicators.
9. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
10. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Capital is both a material stock and an institutionalized claim on future income.
- Investment links present resources to future productive capacity.
- Accumulation expands capacity only when investment exceeds depreciation and maintenance needs.
- Capital intensity can support productivity without determining who captures gains.
- NPV depends strongly on the discount rate and the distribution of future costs and benefits.
- Finance directs accumulation; it can fund productive capacity, speculation, extraction, or resilience.
- Maintenance is part of accumulation, not a residual expense.
- Sustainable investment must account for ecological limits, resilience, public value, and intergenerational obligations.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with national accounts, infrastructure inventories, firm balance sheets, investment-flow data, public capital stock data, maintenance-backlog estimates, financial-flow data, carbon accounts, and public project appraisal datasets.
