# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models information, uncertainty, and imperfect markets as problems of hidden quality, hidden action, costly search, signaling, screening, credibility, disclosure, consumer opacity, and robust decision-making under deep uncertainty.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized market-quality and credit-risk type distributions.
2. Simulates adverse selection through pooled expected value and high-quality exit.
3. Models moral hazard as hidden effort under insurance or risk shifting.
4. Compares signal credibility when signaling costs differ by type.
5. Evaluates the net value of information search under rising acquisition and processing costs.
6. Models consumer opacity from complexity, hidden fees, contract length, and trust.
7. Compares actions under multiple uncertainty scenarios using expected value and worst-case robustness.
8. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
9. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Markets coordinate knowledge through prices, but prices depend on the information structure beneath them.
- Asymmetric information can degrade market quality even when actors behave strategically.
- Adverse selection occurs before agreement when hidden characteristics shape market participation.
- Moral hazard occurs after agreement when hidden action changes behavior.
- Signaling and screening make hidden quality, effort, or risk more legible.
- Disclosure must be effectively intelligible, not merely formally available.
- Deep uncertainty requires robustness, precaution, stress testing, and epistemic humility.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with market-level data, consumer complaint data, credit records, insurance datasets, regulatory disclosures, audit records, product quality evidence, supervisory data, or field-experiment evidence on disclosure and screening.
