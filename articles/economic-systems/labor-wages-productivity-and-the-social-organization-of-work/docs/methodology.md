# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models labor, wages, productivity, and the social organization of work as problems of output, compensation, bargaining power, institutional support, care burdens, time allocation, wage adequacy, employment quality, and technological change.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized sector labor scenarios.
2. Calculates labor productivity, wage share, average wage, and unit labor cost.
3. Simulates wage-productivity divergence over time.
4. Evaluates social reproduction adequacy using wages, transfers, household costs, and care costs.
5. Evaluates time poverty through paid work, care, household maintenance, commute time, and rest.
6. Simulates wage outcomes under bargaining-power and institutional-support scenarios.
7. Models automation shocks across output, labor demand, wage share, and employment quality.
8. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
9. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Labor is not merely an input; it is a social relation carried by human beings with needs, time limits, and care obligations.
- Productivity creates distributive possibility, but institutions determine how gains are shared.
- Wages are both prices and social institutions linking production to household survival.
- Bargaining power, labor law, unions, social insurance, and macroeconomic conditions shape wage outcomes.
- Care and reproductive labor sustain paid work but are often undercounted or undervalued.
- Time poverty can exist even when employment and income are formally adequate.
- Automation can augment labor, displace labor, deskill work, or intensify managerial control depending on institutional context.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with labor-force surveys, establishment data, national accounts, time-use surveys, wage records, unionization data, household cost data, occupational safety data, and sector-level productivity measures.
