# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion represents households, firms, markets, and states as interdependent institutional actors within an economic system.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized actor metadata.
2. Builds institutional flow scenarios for households, firms, and states.
3. Calculates household saving, firm profit, public borrowing, and reproduction indicators.
4. Creates market-access scenarios based on income, price, institutional access, and need.
5. Builds risk-distribution scenarios that assign shocks across households, firms, markets, and states.
6. Simulates institutional reproduction dynamics over time.
7. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
8. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Households are not only consumers; they reproduce labor and absorb risk.
- Firms are not only production units; they are centers of investment, employment, power, and strategy.
- Markets are not natural orders; they are institutional arenas requiring law, money, infrastructure, and trust.
- States are not external to the economy; they constitute legal order, public goods, redistribution, stabilization, and long-range capacity.
- System reproduction depends on household stability, productive capacity, and institutional capacity together.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with household survey data, firm-level datasets, public finance accounts, labor-market data, national accounts, financial accounts, and environmental indicators.
