# Socialism, Planning, and the Mixed Economy

This folder is the article-level research package for:

**Socialism, Planning, and the Mixed Economy**

It is designed as an economist-grade companion workflow for studying mixed-economy structure, market allocation, public planning, public ownership, social rights, decommodification, industrial policy, infrastructure coordination, public utilities, welfare states, planning capacity, crisis coordination, globalization constraints, democratic accountability, ecological transition, and sustainable systems.

## Standard Economic Systems Stack

This repository standardizes Economic Systems article companions around:

- **Python** — mixed-economy composition, planning capacity, public-ownership shares, decommodification, social-rights scoring, industrial policy, public utility governance, crisis coordination, climate-transition coordination, globalization constraints, visualization
- **R** — mixed-economy summaries, public/private composition graphics, planning-capacity indicators, decommodification and crisis-coordination comparisons
- **Stata** — applied mixed-economy and planning-capacity replication workflows and indicator tables
- **SQL** — structured market, planning, ownership, public-services, industrial-policy, utility, welfare-state, crisis, globalization, and sustainability metadata
- **Julia** — numerical simulations for allocation mixes, public investment coordination, planning capacity, crisis scaling, decommodification, and sustainable-transition trajectories

## Research Questions

1. How do modern economies combine markets, planning, public provision, regulation, and social rights?
2. How does socialism differ from social democracy, public economy, and mixed-economy settlements?
3. Which sectors are better suited to market allocation, public provision, public ownership, or strategic planning?
4. How does planning capacity depend on state capacity, data quality, institutional reach, feedback, and democratic accountability?
5. How do public services and social insurance decommodify basic needs?
6. How do public ownership and utility governance affect access, investment, and universal-service obligations?
7. How does industrial policy coordinate structural transformation?
8. How do crisis, war, public health, and ecological transition reveal the need for planning?
9. How does globalization constrain or reshape national planning capacity?
10. How can mixed economies be judged by resilience, legitimacy, equality, capability, and ecological transition?
11. How can Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia make mixed-economy assumptions transparent?

## Notes

This is a stylized, reproducible teaching and research scaffold. It is not an official public-sector national accounts model, public-ownership registry, planning authority dataset, legal analysis, socialist-economy classification system, or policy-advice tool. Its purpose is to make the article's conceptual claims computationally inspectable.
