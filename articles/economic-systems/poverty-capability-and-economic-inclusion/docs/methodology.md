# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models poverty, capability, and economic inclusion as interacting income, deprivation, conversion, service, work, housing, finance, care, child-development, spatial, digital, and vulnerability systems.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized household, multidimensional deprivation, capability, inclusion, work/informality, housing/infrastructure, finance, care, child/youth, spatial, public-service, digital-access, and vulnerability scenarios.
2. Calculates poverty rates and poverty gaps.
3. Scores multidimensional deprivation across health, education, housing, sanitation, electricity, food, transport, digital access, safety, and institutional access.
4. Evaluates capability through income, health, education, mobility, safety, time, and institutional access.
5. Models conversion conditions that determine how resources become real freedom.
6. Distinguishes formal inclusion from durable inclusion.
7. Measures vulnerability through savings, debt, insecure work, weak services, and shock exposure.
8. Evaluates public services and social protection as capability-building infrastructure.
9. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
10. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Poverty is not only low income; it is also deprivation, vulnerability, insecurity, and restricted capability.
- Poverty depth matters as well as poverty incidence.
- Capability depends on conversion conditions, including health, infrastructure, safety, institutions, time, and social recognition.
- Economic inclusion is meaningful only when participation is secure enough to build capability.
- Work can be inclusionary or precarious depending on wages, protections, stability, and recognition.
- Financial inclusion can be empowering or extractive.
- Housing, infrastructure, and place shape practical freedom.
- Digital systems increasingly define access to work, benefits, education, finance, and services.
- Public services and social protection are productive capability-building institutions.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with household surveys, multidimensional poverty datasets, labor-force surveys, administrative data, benefit records, health and education indicators, housing and infrastructure data, spatial opportunity metrics, digital-access data, and social-protection records.
