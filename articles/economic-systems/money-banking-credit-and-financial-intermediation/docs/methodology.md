# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models money, banking, credit, and financial intermediation as balance-sheet systems that organize purchasing power, liquidity, debt obligations, credit allocation, and systemic risk.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized money aggregate and bank balance-sheet scenarios.
2. Calculates money supply, leverage, loan-to-deposit ratios, and capital ratios.
3. Simulates deposit creation through bank lending.
4. Evaluates debt-service capacity using interest coverage and debt-service ratios.
5. Evaluates liquidity stress using HQLA, cash outflows, and liquidity coverage.
6. Scores credit allocation across productive, speculative, household, public, and ecological uses.
7. Scores monetary hierarchy and collateral access.
8. Evaluates sustainable finance direction.
9. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
10. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Money is an institutional claim accepted for payment and settlement.
- Deposits are central to modern money.
- Bank lending can create deposit liabilities and expand purchasing power.
- Leverage magnifies both returns and fragility.
- Liquidity is distinct from solvency and matters under stress.
- Credit allocation matters as much as credit volume.
- Financial inclusion should be judged by quality, fairness, and resilience, not debt access alone.
- Sustainable finance asks what futures credit makes possible.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with central-bank data, commercial bank balance sheets, supervisory data, household debt datasets, firm financial statements, payment-system data, credit-allocation data, and climate-finance or transition-risk datasets.
