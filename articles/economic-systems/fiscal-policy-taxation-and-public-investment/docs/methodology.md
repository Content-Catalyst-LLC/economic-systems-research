# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models fiscal policy, taxation, and public investment as interacting revenue, expenditure, debt, distributional, maintenance, territorial, and resilience systems.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized fiscal-position, tax-distribution, spending-composition, multiplier, maintenance, local-fiscal-capacity, and public-investment-resilience scenarios.
2. Calculates budget balances, primary balances, tax ratios, deficit ratios, interest costs, and next-period debt.
3. Evaluates effective tax rates and progressivity across household income groups.
4. Separates current expenditure from public investment and social transfers.
5. Estimates multiplier-adjusted output effects across fiscal instruments.
6. Calculates infrastructure maintenance gaps and cumulative deferred maintenance.
7. Evaluates local fiscal capacity and territorial service gaps.
8. Scores public investment options using avoided-loss and resilience-return logic.
9. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
10. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Fiscal policy is not only short-run stabilization; it is one of the main ways societies build collective capacity.
- Taxation is not only revenue collection; it also shapes distribution, incentives, legitimacy, and state capacity.
- Deficits and debt must be interpreted in context, including interest rates, growth, purpose, and institutional credibility.
- Public investment can crowd in productive private activity by reducing bottlenecks and uncertainty.
- Maintenance gaps are hidden public liabilities.
- Automatic stabilizers protect households and stabilize demand.
- Local fiscal capacity shapes territorial inequality.
- Sustainable public finance requires fiscal systems capable of maintaining infrastructure, funding adaptation, and investing ahead of crisis.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with national accounts, budget data, tax records, household survey microdata, public investment datasets, infrastructure condition assessments, municipal finance data, debt service schedules, and climate-adaptation cost-benefit studies.
