# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models trade, globalization, and uneven development as interacting trade, production, finance, currency, labor, regional, ecological, technological, and resilience systems.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized trade-position, export-basket, value-chain, commodity-dependence, capital-flow, regional-inequality, labor-exposure, ecological-burden, and strategic-resilience scenarios.
2. Calculates trade openness, trade balance, and import dependence.
3. Calculates export concentration and diversification scores.
4. Measures domestic value capture in gross exports.
5. Scores value-chain position and upgrading potential.
6. Evaluates commodity dependence and terms-of-trade vulnerability.
7. Assesses currency and capital-flow exposure.
8. Compares regional divergence under globalization.
9. Measures labor exposure to import competition and offshoring.
10. Scores ecological burden displacement and sustainable trade resilience.
11. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
12. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Trade volume alone does not indicate developmental quality.
- Trade deficits and surpluses must be interpreted structurally, not morally.
- Export concentration can increase vulnerability to price, climate, and geopolitical shocks.
- Domestic value capture matters more than gross export participation alone.
- Global value chains contain hierarchies of design, finance, intellectual property, logistics, assembly, extraction, and ecological burden.
- Financial openness can discipline trade openness through capital-flow volatility, currency hierarchy, and foreign-currency debt.
- Globalization produces territorial winners and losers within countries as well as between countries.
- Ecological costs can be displaced through supply chains.
- Sustainable trade requires capability, diversification, resilience, and ecological accountability.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with trade flows, input-output tables, domestic value-added data, customs data, export concentration measures, regional labor-market data, exchange-rate and capital-flow data, commodity price series, emissions embodied in trade, logistics data, foreign direct investment data, and global value-chain databases.
