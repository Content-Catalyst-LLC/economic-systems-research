# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models economic systems within planetary boundaries as interacting boundary-pressure, resource-use, sector-throughput, coupled-system, ecological-space, transition-capacity, finance-direction, boundary-aware-accounting, sufficiency, stewardship, and resilience systems.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized planetary-boundary pressure scenarios.
2. Calculates boundary pressure ratios, overshoot status, and priority scores.
3. Models resource use through population, affluence, and resource intensity.
4. Evaluates sector pressure across energy, food, industry, housing, transport, public infrastructure, and materials.
5. Models coupled energy-food-land-water pressure.
6. Evaluates justice and unequal ecological space across country groups and social groups.
7. Scores innovation, efficiency, rebound, sufficiency, and redesign scenarios.
8. Evaluates transition capacity through state capacity, public investment, social legitimacy, technological capability, institutional coordination, and adaptive governance.
9. Scores finance direction toward either overshoot reinforcement or boundary-aware development.
10. Evaluates boundary-aware accounting using GDP, material throughput, ecological pressure, natural capital, inclusion, and wellbeing.
11. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
12. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Economies are subsystems of the Earth system.
- Planetary boundaries describe safe operating space, not marginal environmental side effects.
- Boundary pressure depends on total scale, not only efficiency per unit.
- Resource use can be decomposed into population, affluence, and resource intensity.
- Coupled systems matter because solving one boundary problem can intensify another.
- Justice is central because ecological pressure and ecological harm are unequally distributed.
- Innovation matters, but efficiency without scale discipline can fail.
- State capacity and public investment are transition conditions.
- Finance matters because capital allocation builds either overshoot or resilience.
- Measurement must connect output, wellbeing, ecological pressure, and future viability.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with planetary-boundary datasets, material-flow accounts, energy balances, land-use data, freshwater data, nutrient-flow data, emissions inventories, ecological-footprint data, national accounts, household distribution data, environmental justice data, and transition-investment data.
