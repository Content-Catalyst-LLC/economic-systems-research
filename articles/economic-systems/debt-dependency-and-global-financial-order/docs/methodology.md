# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models debt, dependency, and global financial order as interacting fiscal, external, monetary, legal, institutional, social, and developmental systems.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized debt-position, currency-hierarchy, external-account, credit-discipline, conditionality, creditor-power, austerity, commodity-debt, capital-flow, restructuring, and sustainable-finance scenarios.
2. Calculates debt-to-output ratios, debt-service ratios, FX debt burdens, reserve adequacy, and interest-growth pressures.
3. Evaluates currency hierarchy and foreign-currency exposure.
4. Measures external vulnerability through import dependence, reserve buffers, capital-flow volatility, and external financing need.
5. Compares sovereign credit discipline across ratings, spreads, rollover needs, and market access.
6. Scores conditionality and policy-space pressure.
7. Models austerity adjustment burdens and public-capacity erosion.
8. Evaluates commodity-linked debt vulnerability.
9. Simulates sudden-stop exposure and reserve losses.
10. Compares restructuring options.
11. Scores sustainable development finance across productive investment, resilience, social protection, and debt manageability.
12. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
13. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Debt size alone does not determine vulnerability.
- External debt must be interpreted relative to export earnings, currency denomination, maturity, and reserves.
- Foreign-currency debt can become destabilizing under exchange-rate depreciation.
- Interest-growth differentials matter for debt dynamics.
- Reserve adequacy and external liquidity are central to crisis resilience.
- Debt sustainability is not only fiscal; it is also developmental, social, external, and political.
- Dependency is cumulative: export concentration, imported essentials, foreign-currency debt, external finance reliance, and weak domestic capability can reinforce one another.
- Restructuring is a political economy problem of loss distribution.
- Sustainable finance must build future capacity rather than merely preserve repayment.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with debt stocks by currency and maturity, external debt service, export earnings, reserve data, commodity price data, capital-flow data, sovereign spreads, credit ratings, fiscal data, public spending composition, social indicators, and restructuring terms.
