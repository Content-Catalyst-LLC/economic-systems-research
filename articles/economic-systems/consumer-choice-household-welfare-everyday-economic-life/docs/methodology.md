# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion represents consumer choice and household welfare as broader than preference satisfaction under a simple budget constraint.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized household profiles with income, transfers, liquid buffers, debt service, fixed burdens, and care burdens.
2. Creates spending categories for essentials and discretionary items.
3. Builds inflation scenarios affecting rent, food, transport, utilities, health, and other spending.
4. Calculates saving, essentials ratios, fragility ratios, and effective command over essentials.
5. Calculates time-poverty indicators from paid labor, care work, commuting, household logistics, and rest.
6. Models public-goods scenarios that reduce private expense and time burdens.
7. Simulates asset depletion under household stress.
8. Exports figures, tables, and a SQLite database.
9. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Consumer choice is feasible action under constraint, not pure preference expression.
- Household welfare includes consumption, housing security, health access, time, care, public goods, assets, and resilience.
- Essentials inflation affects welfare differently from discretionary-price changes.
- Public goods alter the real content of household choice by reducing private burdens.
- Debt and fixed obligations narrow the feasible set before ordinary choice begins.
- Time poverty can coexist with monetary solvency.
- Observed consumption does not necessarily reveal well-being.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with household expenditure surveys, time-use data, local price indices, debt-service data, public-benefit data, and regional cost-of-living measures.
