# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models growth, development, and structural transformation as interacting output, productivity, sectoral, capability, spatial, trade, distributional, ecological, and financial systems.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized growth-path, sectoral transformation, capability, infrastructure, trade, inequality, energy, and debt-fragility scenarios.
2. Calculates output growth, cumulative growth, and labor productivity.
3. Calculates sectoral output and labor shares across agriculture, industry, services, and knowledge sectors.
4. Estimates within-sector productivity contributions and labor-reallocation contributions.
5. Constructs a multidimensional development capability index.
6. Evaluates urbanization, infrastructure depth, and spatial inclusion.
7. Evaluates export concentration and diversification.
8. Evaluates inequality and inclusion as developmental conditions.
9. Calculates energy intensity and ecological pressure indicators.
10. Scores debt, external exposure, and middle-income fragility risks.
11. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
12. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Growth measures aggregate scale; development measures broader capability.
- Structural transformation changes what an economy produces and how it is organized.
- Productivity growth depends on learning, infrastructure, management, institutions, and technology.
- Development can stall when labor moves into low-productivity services or when industry fails to deepen.
- Growth without development can occur through extraction, debt, speculative booms, or narrow export dependence.
- Infrastructure and urbanization are spatial foundations of development.
- Inequality and exclusion are not only moral problems; they can weaken developmental durability.
- Energy intensity and ecological pressure alter the meaning of growth under planetary constraints.
- Financial dependence, debt exposure, and narrow export bases can reduce developmental sovereignty.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with national accounts, sectoral employment data, labor-productivity series, household survey data, infrastructure indicators, human development indicators, energy balances, emissions data, export concentration measures, external debt data, and public investment records.
