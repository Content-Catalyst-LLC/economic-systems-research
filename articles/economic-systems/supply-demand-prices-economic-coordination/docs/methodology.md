# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion represents supply, demand, prices, and coordination as a set of transparent market-equilibrium and institutional-access workflows.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized market scenarios for baseline, demand expansion, supply shock, rigid supply, elastic supply, and market-power cases.
2. Solves linear supply-demand equilibrium for each scenario.
3. Calculates price and quantity changes relative to baseline.
4. Calculates point elasticities of demand and supply at equilibrium.
5. Models effective demand as need filtered through income, credit, prices, and institutional access.
6. Compares private market price with marginal social cost when external costs are present.
7. Exports figures, tables, and a SQLite database.
8. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Prices coordinate decentralized decisions, but only within institutional and distributional conditions.
- Demand is not need; it is need backed by purchasing power and access.
- Supply reflects productive capacity, infrastructure, labor, energy, finance, logistics, and ecological constraints.
- Elasticities determine whether adjustment occurs mainly through prices or quantities.
- Externalities and public goods show why price signals can be socially incomplete.
- Market clearing is not the same as social adequacy, justice, resilience, or ecological viability.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized linear equations for transparency. Applied analysis should extend it with sector-specific data, microdata, time-series price data, market structure indicators, cost curves, supply-chain data, and social-cost estimates.
