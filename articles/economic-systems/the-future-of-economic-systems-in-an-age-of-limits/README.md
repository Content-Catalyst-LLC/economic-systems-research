# The Future of Economic Systems in an Age of Limits

This folder is the article-level research package for:

**The Future of Economic Systems in an Age of Limits**

It is designed as an economist-grade companion workflow for studying future viability, ecological pressure, institutional capacity, social inclusion, resilience, throughput pressure, transition capacity, public investment, technology governance, social trust, well-being beyond output, circularity, repair systems, finance redirection, asymmetric responsibility, post-growth strategy, and sustainable economic futures.

## Standard Economic Systems Stack

This repository standardizes Economic Systems article companions around:

- **Python** — future-viability scoring, throughput-pressure identities, transition-capacity indicators, well-being dashboards, finance-redirection analysis, circularity and repair scoring, asymmetric-responsibility metrics, resilience and legitimacy modeling, visualization
- **R** — future-systems dashboards, resource-pressure summaries, transition-capacity graphics, well-being and inclusion comparison tables
- **Stata** — applied future-systems indicator replication workflows and scenario-table analysis
- **SQL** — structured future-viability, throughput, transition, finance, technology, circularity, justice, legitimacy, and resilience metadata
- **Julia** — numerical simulations for future-viability trajectories, throughput pressure, transition capacity, resilience, and ecological-policy scenario dynamics

## Research Questions

1. Which economic-system scenarios remain viable when ecological pressure, institutional capacity, social inclusion, and resilience are considered together?
2. How do population, affluence, and material intensity shape future throughput pressure?
3. What transition capacities are needed to move from fossil-intensive, extractive systems toward boundary-aware systems?
4. How do public investment, policy credibility, technology, social trust, and implementation capacity interact?
5. How should future economic performance be measured beyond GDP?
6. How do finance, risk, and capital allocation steer development toward either stranded assets or durable systems?
7. How do circularity, repair, maintenance, and regeneration reduce material pressure?
8. How do global inequality and asymmetric obligation shape future economic pathways?
9. How do democratic legitimacy and public trust affect transition durability?
10. What distinguishes a system that produces output from a system that remains livable, governable, and materially viable over time?

## Core Model Ideas

```text
Future viability:
FV = f(ecological pressure, institutional capacity, social inclusion, resilience)
```

```text
Throughput pressure:
TP = Population × Affluence × Material Intensity
```

```text
Transition capacity:
TC = f(public investment, policy credibility, technology, social trust, implementation capacity)
```

```text
Well-being beyond output:
WB = f(health, security, inclusion, ecological quality, public goods)
```

```text
Finance direction:
FD = f(resilience investment, restoration, public goods alignment, fossil exposure, short-termism)
```

```text
Democratic transition legitimacy:
DL = f(fairness, affordability, voice, trust, visible benefit)
```

## Notes

This is a stylized, reproducible teaching and research scaffold. It is not an official macroeconomic forecast, IPCC pathway, UNEP resource model, OECD policy model, financial-risk assessment, legal analysis, or policy-advice tool. Its purpose is to make the article's conceptual claims computationally inspectable.
