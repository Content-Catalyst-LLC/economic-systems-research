# Methodology

## Purpose

This article companion models macroeconomic stability, business cycles, and crisis as interacting demand, employment, financial, household, public-capacity, and external-shock systems.

The workflow:

1. Creates stylized aggregate-demand and business-cycle scenarios.
2. Calculates output, output gaps, demand shares, and investment volatility.
3. Evaluates household debt-service stress and balance-sheet vulnerability.
4. Simulates credit contraction and debt-deflation pressure.
5. Models fiscal stabilization using multipliers and automatic stabilizers.
6. Connects output growth to unemployment through an Okun-style relation.
7. Evaluates inflation-unemployment-policy tradeoffs under different shock types.
8. Evaluates open-economy shock transmission through export, import, exchange-rate, and capital-flow channels.
9. Models recovery paths under austerity, neutral stabilization, public investment, and resilience-oriented recovery.
10. Exports tables, figures, and a SQLite database.
11. Replicates the logic across Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Julia.

## Key Concepts

- Macroeconomic stability is a public good, not merely low inflation.
- Business cycles arise from interacting decisions under uncertainty.
- Aggregate demand, investment expectations, credit, inventories, and policy interact cumulatively.
- Balance sheets matter because debt stocks can amplify output-flow shocks.
- Fiscal policy and automatic stabilizers can dampen downturns when private demand retrenches.
- Monetary policy supports liquidity and financial conditions but cannot substitute for every stabilizing institution.
- Household security is a macroeconomic stabilizer.
- Sustainable macroeconomic order must preserve public capacity, infrastructure, ecological adaptation, and long-run resilience.

## Limitations

The workflow uses stylized synthetic data for teaching and article support. Applied analysis should extend it with national accounts, labor-market data, inflation data, household survey data, credit aggregates, public-finance records, central-bank data, external-sector data, and business-cycle dating datasets.
